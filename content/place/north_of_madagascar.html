North of Madagascar

The city of Antsiranana, also known as Diego-Suarez, is the largest city in northern Madagascar. While its colonial-style buildings add to its charm, what truly sets it apart is its warm and peaceful atmosphere. Rich in history and cultural events, a visit to Antsiranana promises a journey of discovery. Exploring the white sandy beaches of its many bays is an experience not to be missed.

Emerald Sea

Almost otherworldly in its beauty, the Emerald Sea is one of the most stunning landscapes in the world. Its breathtaking panorama, combined with the trade winds of the Varatraza, makes it an ideal spot for windsurfing and kitesurfing. The rich underwater world also invites visitors to enjoy a diving excursion. Access to the Emerald Sea is via traditional sailing boat, and while the journey may involve a bit of ocean spray, the reward is worth it. Upon arrival on Suarez Island, you can take a refreshing swim in the turquoise waters while the staff prepares a delicious grilled fish lunch.

French Mountain

French Mountain is a nature reserve that dominates the eastern part of Diego-Suarez. A climb to its heights offers a breathtaking panoramic view of Cape Amber and the Emerald Sea. The reserve is also home to majestic baobab trees, making it a must-visit for nature lovers.

Amber Mountain National Park

Located near the small town of Joffreville, about an hour’s drive from Antsiranana, Amber Mountain National Park boasts a unique ecosystem nestled within a volcanic massif at an altitude of 1,475 meters. While part of the park is reserved for scientific research, visitors can embark on guided hikes to explore the accessible areas. This destination is highly recommended for those who appreciate nature and adventure.

Ramena

Ramena is a charming fishing village situated 20 km from Diego-Suarez. Known for its stunning beach and laid-back atmosphere, it’s the perfect place to relax and enjoy fresh seafood at the local beachside restaurants. Ramena also serves as the departure point for boat trips to the Emerald Sea.

Three Bays

The trio of bays in Diego-Suarez offers an awe-inspiring coastal spectacle. With their powdery white sand and crystal-clear waters, Sakalava Bay, Dunes Bay, and Pigeons Bay are ideal spots for swimming, strolling, and soaking in the natural beauty of Madagascar’s northern shores.



The Red Tsingy of Diégo are breathtaking geological wonders. Unlike other Tsingy formations in Madagascar, these striking red, pink, and white needle-like structures are not made of limestone but are instead composed of a unique blend of sandstone, marl, and limestone. Offering a stunning backdrop for photography, a visit to the Red Tsingy is also a perfect way to conclude a scenic road trip through the winding trails of the Analamerana Park savannah.
